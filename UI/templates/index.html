Introduction

    Introduces the setting, characters, and conflict.
    Establishes the mood and tone of the story.
    Provides background information and context for the story.

Rising Action

    The event or catalyst that sets the main plot in motion.
    Creates a problem, challenge, or conflict that the characters must address.
    Builds tension and suspense as the story progresses when introducing complications and obstactles

Climax

    The turning point or the most intense moment in the story.
    The protagonist faces a critical decision or confronts the main conflict.
    Often represents the moment of greatest tension or emotional intensity

Falling Action

    Deals with the aftermath of the climax
    Resolves the remaining conflicts or unanswered questions
    Begins to wind dow the story and move towards a resolution

Conclusion

    The final outcome or conclusion of the story.
    Loose ends are tied up, and the main conflict is usually resolved
    Offers a sense of closure and fulfillment.
